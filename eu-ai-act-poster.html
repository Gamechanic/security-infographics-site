01
What it is

The world's first comprehensive, risk-based AI law — Regulation (EU) 2024/1689. It sorts AI systems into tiers, and the higher the risk, the heavier the duties. Entered into force Aug 2024; obligations switch on in phases.

Not one rule for all AI — the tier decides what you must do.

02
Who it reaches
🌍
Extraterritorial — applies wherever you're based if your AI's output is used in the EU.
🏗️
Providers & deployers — building, importing, or putting an AI system into use both count.

Being outside the EU is not an exemption — the EU market is the trigger.

03
The four risk tiers — top = most restricted · the vast majority of AI is minimal-risk

Classify every system by tier first — that single call decides whether it is banned, heavily regulated, must disclose, or free of new duties.

04
Unacceptable · banned

A short list of AI uses judged an unacceptable threat to rights — prohibited outright:

🚫
Social scoring — rating people by behaviour for unrelated decisions.
🧠
Subliminal manipulation — exploiting vulnerabilities to distort behaviour.
📸
Untargeted face scraping · emotion inference at work / school.
👁️
Real-time public biometric ID — by police, save narrow exceptions.
05
High · heavy obligations

Allowed, but only with a full compliance programme. Covers hiring, credit scoring, biometrics, critical infrastructure, medical devices.

risk managementdata governancetechnical docshuman oversightaccuracy · robustnessconformity assessment

Prove it before you ship — and keep proving it in use.

06
Timeline of application — phased in · dates being amended (2026 "Digital Omnibus")

Bans land first, then GPAI, then high-risk — the tail is shifting, so always check the live text.

07
Limited · transparency

Lower-risk systems carry one core duty: tell people it's AI.

chatbots· deepfakes· GenAI output

Disclose that content is AI-generated — and that they're talking to a machine.

08
Minimal · everything else

The vast majority of AI — spam filters, recommenders, game AI — falls here.

✅
No new obligations — existing law (privacy, consumer, safety) still applies.

Voluntary codes of conduct are encouraged, not required.

09
GPAI — a separate track — general-purpose models sit alongside the risk tiers

🧩 All GPAI

Technical documentation · instructions for downstream use · copyright compliance · a public summary of training data.

⚡ Systemic risk

Trained with > 10²⁵ FLOPs of compute. Adds: model evaluations · adversarial testing.

🛡️ & more

Systemic-risk models also owe serious-incident reporting and cybersecurity protections.

"GPAI" is its own duty set — a model can be GPAI and be built into a high-risk system.

10
Penalties

Fines scale to the breach — the top band is reserved for the banned uses:

ViolationUp to
Prohibited practices€35M or 7% turnover
Other obligationslower bands apply

7% is of global annual turnover — whichever figure is higher.

11
Watch out
🕰️
Dates are shifting — the timeline is being amended; verify against the current consolidated text.
🧩
GPAI ≠ high-risk — a distinct track; a system can be caught by both.
💬
"Low-risk" still discloses — transparency duties reach chatbots & deepfakes too.
12
The takeaway
Classify every AI system by risk tier, then meet that tier's duties — unacceptable is banned, high is heavily regulated, limited must disclose.
Inventory→ Classify by tier→ Meet the duties→ Keep documentation

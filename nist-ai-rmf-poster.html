01
What it is

The NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0, NIST AI 100-1, Jan 2023) — voluntary guidance to identify, assess & manage AI risk across the entire lifecycle, from design to deployment to retirement.

A framework, not a checklist — it shapes how you think about AI risk, it doesn't hand you a pass/fail list.

02
Why it exists
🎯
Risk is contextual — the same model can be low-risk in one use and high-risk in another.
🔁
Risk is continuous — AI drifts; risk must be managed across the whole lifecycle, not once.

Voluntary & rights-preserving — usable by any org, any sector, any size.

03
The four core functions — GOVERN wraps the cycle of MAP → MEASURE → MANAGE

Govern is the culture & accountability layer that surrounds and feeds the ongoing loop of Map (frame the risk), Measure (test it), and Manage (respond to it).

04
What "trustworthy" means — the characteristics the framework targets
valid & reliable safe secure & resilient accountable & transparent explainable & interpretable privacy-enhanced fair — bias managed

Valid & reliable is foundational — the rest are the qualities you trade off and balance in context.

05
Govern is the foundation

Without governance — an AI inventory, policy, and clear ownership — the other three functions don't stick.

inventory→ policy→ ownership

Culture is the hard part — and the part everything else rests on.

06
Measure = where testing lives

The Measure function is where you prove — not assume — that risk is controlled:

evaluationsadversarial testingred-teamingdrift monitoring

Each metric needs a threshold and an owner — otherwise it's measurement theater.

07
The Generative AI Profile — NIST AI 600-1 · July 2024 · the GenAI companion

A companion profile — not a new framework. It names the risks unique to generative AI and points to concrete actions under each of the four functions.

08
How to adopt it — start with Govern, then run the loop

Then iterate — it's a loop, not a launch. Re-map and re-measure as the system and its context change.

09

10
Watch out
📋
Reading it as a control list — it's a framework; it guides thinking, it doesn't grade you.
🏛️
Skipping Govern — culture is the hard part, and the rest won't stick without it.
🤖
GenAI without 600-1 — generative AI needs the companion profile, not RMF 1.0 alone.
🎭
Metrics without owners — measurement with no thresholds or owners is theater.
11
The takeaway
The NIST AI RMF is a repeatable loop — Govern the culture, then Map · Measure · Manage the risk — to build trustworthy AI, GenAI included.
Govern→ Map→ Measure→ Manage↺

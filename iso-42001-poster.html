01
What it is

ISO/IEC 42001:2023 (published Dec 2023) is the world's first AI management system (AIMS) standard — and it's certifiable. Like ISO/IEC 27001 for security, an organization can be independently audited and certified against it.

It certifies how you govern AI — not one model, but your whole AI operation.

02
What an “AIMS” is

A management system to establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve how the organization governs AI:

policiesrolesprocessescontrols

A repeatable system, owned by leadership — not a one-off review or a pile of documents.

03
The Plan-Do-Check-Act loop — the engine of every management system

Governance isn't a project you finish — it's a cycle you keep running as your AI, your risks, and the rules around you change.

04
Core requirements — the clauses you're audited against
🧭
Leadership & AI policy — top management owns responsible AI.
📈
Objectives & planning — set measurable AI goals, plan to meet them.
⚠️
AI risk assessment & treatment — identify, then reduce the risk.
🛠️
Operational controls — run the safeguards day to day.
👥
AI system impact assessment — effects on people & society.
📊
Performance evaluation — monitor, measure, management review.
🔍
Internal audit — check yourself before the certifier does.
🔁
Continual improvement — close gaps, raise the bar each cycle.
05
Annex A controls — AI-specific

Beyond the clauses, Annex A lists controls unique to AI:

data quality & provenanceAI system lifecycletransparency to usershuman oversightresourcingthird-party / supplier

Pick the controls your risk assessment justifies — and record why.

06
AI impact assessment
🏢
Not just the org — most standards weigh risk to the business.
🌍
To people & society — 42001 asks how the AI affects individuals and society.

A distinctive AIMS requirement — look outward, not only inward.

07
Why certify

Certification turns a claim into audited evidence — governance that customers, partners, and regulators can trust.

“we're responsible”→ independently audited→ proof

Evidence beats assertion — especially in procurement and due diligence.

Related ISO/IEC standards

42001 is the certifiable core; these support it with deeper how-to guidance.

10
Watch out
🏷️
System ≠ safe model — it proves the system exists, not that every model is safe.
🎯
Scope it right — define which AI & teams are covered; a narrow scope proves little.
⚖️
Not a legal shield — it complements the EU AI Act & NIST RMF, doesn't replace them.
🔁
Keep it alive — certification lapses if the loop stops turning.
11
The takeaway
ISO/IEC 42001 is the certifiable backbone for running AI responsibly — Plan-Do-Check-Act, with AI-specific controls and impact assessment.
Set the AIMS→ Operate controls→ Audit & improve→ Certify the system
